Iron Horse

Football Club

Community-owned · Cooperatively governed

Iron Horse Football Club is a community-owned, cooperatively governed football club rooted in Kitchener–Waterloo.

We play on the Haldimand Tract — land granted to the Six Nations of the Grand River in 1784 and systematically diminished ever since. That history shapes how we operate.

This region is growing fast and unevenly. People arrive from everywhere and try to build a life in a place where housing is scarce and wages are precarious. Kids need somewhere to go. Families need a reason to feel like this city is theirs.

Iron Horse FC exists for them.

Named for the Iron Horse Trail — a decommissioned rail corridor transformed into shared public space. Like that trail, we take a model built on exclusion and profit and remake it as something accessible, democratic, and ours.

Founding Principles
I
Accessibility over accumulationSliding-scale fees, equipment sharing, and free programming. No one is excluded because they can't pay.
II
Democratic self-governanceOne member, one vote. An elected board. Decisions about the club are made by the people in it.
III
Newcomer & refugee inclusionProgramming, communication, and culture built around the families remaking this region as home.
IV
Indigenous solidarityReciprocal relationships with Six Nations and Indigenous communities, guided by their priorities.
V
Housing justice & class politicsThe cost of living determines who can participate in community life. That's a club concern, not just a political one.
VI
Environmental responsibilityStewardship of the Grand River watershed in our operations, programming, and advocacy.
VII
Art & cultureCreative expression is part of club life — not an add-on. Match days are community gatherings.
VIII
Mutual aidEveryone has something to give and something to receive. The club looks after its own.
IX
Anti-racism & anti-oppressionActive commitment against discrimination of any kind. Education, intervention, accountability.
